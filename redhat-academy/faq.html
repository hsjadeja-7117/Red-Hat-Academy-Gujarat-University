Frequently Asked Questions

New to Red Hat or to the Academy? Start here. Click a question to expand the answer.

Red Hat is a global enterprise software company that builds open source solutions for businesses, most famously Red Hat Enterprise Linux (RHEL). Instead of selling proprietary software licenses, Red Hat's business model is built around open source collaboration, subscriptions, and support - making community-built software reliable enough for banks, hospitals, and governments to run on.

Red Hat was founded in 1993 and grew alongside the broader Linux and open source movement of the 1990s. Over the following decades it became the leading commercial Linux distribution company, eventually expanding into cloud, container, and automation software, and was acquired by IBM in 2019 while continuing to operate with its own open source-first culture and leadership.

Red Hat Academy is Red Hat's education program for universities and colleges. It gives participating institutions - like ours - free access to official Red Hat curriculum, virtual lab environments, and discounted or included certification exam vouchers, so students can learn the same tools used in real enterprise IT environments.

Members get access to guided curriculum, hands-on lab practice in real Linux environments, a path toward industry-recognized certification exams, and membership in a global community of Red Hat Academy students and open source contributors - plus the peer support of our own campus core team.
